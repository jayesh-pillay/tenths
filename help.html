Frequently Asked Questions

How do I create my first task? ▼
Step-by-step:
  1. Tap "+ New Task" in the sidebar
  2. Enter your task title (e.g., "Complete Chapter 5")
  3. Add a description with specific details
  4. Choose your measuring metric (chapters, hours, pages)
  5. Set a deadline
  6. Tap "Create Task"
What's a "measuring metric"? ▼
A measuring metric is what you're tracking progress by. Common examples include:
  • Chapters - for reading or studying
  • Hours - for work or exercise
  • Pages - for writing or document completion
  • Units - for any custom measurement
  • Items - for checklists
How do I add progress to a task? ▼
Method 1:
  1. Go to your Dashboard
  2. Find the task or spending category
  3. Tap the "+val" input box
  4. Enter how many units you completed
  5. Tap "Add Progress"
Tip: If you accidentally enter too much, our system auto-caps it to your target!
Can I edit a task after creating it? ▼
Yes! You can edit most task details:
  • Tap the task to open it
  • Tap the edit icon (pencil)
  • Modify: title, description, target, or deadline
  • Tap "Save"
Note: You cannot change the measuring metric type after creation.
How do I delete a task? ▼
  1. Open the task
  2. Tap the trash/delete icon
  3. Confirm deletion
Warning: This is permanent and cannot be undone. Consider archiving instead!
What does "On Track" vs "Needs Attention" mean? ▼
Our system automatically analyzes your progress against your deadline. If you are falling behind the required daily average to hit your deadline, we flag it as "Needs Attention" so you stay accountable!
How do I change my password? ▼
Head over to the Profile section from your sidebar, scroll down to Security Settings, and hit "Change Password".
Is my data encrypted? ▼
Yes! All your data is securely stored and protected with AES-256 encryption. Your privacy is our absolute priority and your data is never shared.
Can I export my data? ▼
Data exports (CSV formatting) are currently being developed and will be available in the next major update out of the Profile dashboard!
What happens if I delete my account? ▼
When you delete your account through your Profile settings, all task records, analytics, and personal information are permanently scrubbed from our systems. This action is irreversible.

Troubleshooting

❌ App Crashes Frequently

Problem: Tenths keeps crashing when I open it.

Solutions:
  1. Force close the app (Settings → Apps → Tenths → Force Stop)
  2. Clear app cache (Settings → Apps → Tenths → Storage → Clear Cache)
  3. Restart your phone
  4. Reopen Tenths
  5. If still crashing, uninstall and reinstall the app
If the problem persists, email support@tenths.app

🔄 Data Not Syncing

Problem: My progress updates aren't saving or syncing across devices.

Solutions:
  1. Check your internet connection (WiFi or mobile data)
  2. Sign out → Sign back in
  3. Force close and reopen the app
  4. Check if you're on the latest app version
  5. Try again in a few minutes (server may be syncing)
Still not working? Contact support@tenths.app

📋 Getting Started with Tenths

Welcome to Tenth's! This guide walks you through the essential setup and first steps to get you productive in minutes.

1. Understanding the Dashboard

Your dashboard shows:

  • Welcome Banner: Personalized greeting and motivational message
  • Quick Stats: Total tasks, completion rate, tasks due soon
  • In Progress Section: Your active tasks with current progress
  • Upcoming Deadlines: Tasks due in the next 7 days

2. Creating Your First Task

  1. Tap "+ New Task" button
  2. Enter a clear, specific title (e.g., "Complete Chemistry Chapters 1-3")
  3. Add optional description with more details
  4. Choose your measuring metric (chapters, hours, pages, units)
  5. Set your target number (how many chapters, pages, etc.)
  6. Select a deadline
  7. Tap "Create Task"

📊 Mastering Analytics

Analytics is where you see patterns in your productivity. Learn to use data to improve your performance.

1. Understanding Your Metrics

  • Overall Progress: Average completion % across all active tasks
  • On Time Rate: % of tasks completed by deadline
  • Average Days to Complete: How long tasks typically take you
  • Completion Velocity: How fast you're progressing (units per day)

2. Using the Charts

Analytics includes:

  • Completion Trend: Line chart showing your progress over time
  • Category Breakdown: Pie chart of which categories consume your time
  • Status Distribution: How many tasks are on-track vs behind
  • Weekly Summary: Your productivity by day of week

3. Identifying Patterns

Ask yourself:

  • Which time of day am I most productive?
  • Which task categories do I procrastinate on?
  • Are my targets realistic or too ambitious?
  • What day of the week do I perform best?
  • How much time do I actually have vs. what I'm committing to?

4. Taking Action

  1. Review your analytics weekly
  2. Identify 1-2 areas for improvement
  3. Adjust your targets for next week
  4. Schedule work during your peak hours
  5. Break down procrastination-prone tasks smaller
Pro Tip: Weekly analytics reviews (Sunday evening) are the key to continuous improvement!

⏰ Time Management Tips

Master the art of productivity with these proven time management strategies.

1. Setting Realistic Targets

The #1 mistake: over-committing. Be honest about:

  • How many hours you actually have per day
  • How long similar tasks have taken in the past
  • Your energy levels throughout the day
  • Unexpected interruptions and breaks

Formula: (Available Hours × 0.75) ÷ Task Difficulty = Realistic Tasks per Day

2. The Power of Deadlines

  • Set specific deadlines (not just "finish eventually")
  • Work backwards from your deadline
  • Break big tasks into smaller milestones
  • Create a sense of urgency

3. Building Consistency

  1. Track daily (even if just 5 minutes)
  2. Celebrate small wins
  3. Build a streak (consistency matters more than perfection)
  4. Schedule work at the same time each day
  5. Remove distractions during work sessions

4. The 80/20 Rule

Focus on the 20% of tasks that drive 80% of results. Ask yourself:

  • Which tasks have the most impact?
  • Which will be noticed/rewarded?
  • Which align with my long-term goals?
  • Prioritize these first, then handle the rest
Golden Rule: Under-promise, over-deliver. It's better to finish early and build momentum than to miss deadlines.

🎯 Building Your First Week

A step-by-step framework for your first week of productive work with Tenths.

Sunday Evening: Planning (30 mins)

  1. Identify 3-5 main goals for the week
  2. Break each into measurable milestones
  3. Be specific: "Complete 5 chapters" not "Study"
  4. Set realistic deadlines (spread across the week)
  5. Create tasks in Tenths

Daily: Execution & Tracking (5 mins)

  1. Morning: Check dashboard for today's priorities
  2. Throughout day: Update progress as you complete work
  3. Evening: Log final progress for the day
  4. Celebrate completed tasks!

Wednesday: Mid-Week Check-In (10 mins)

  • Are you on track?
  • If behind: adjust targets or add buffer time
  • If ahead: bonus! Celebrate momentum
  • Any tasks that are taking longer than expected?

Friday: Wrap-Up (15 mins)

  1. Finish as many tasks as possible
  2. Mark completed tasks
  3. Review what you accomplished (feels amazing!)
  4. Note any unfinished tasks for next week

Sunday: Review & Adjust (20 mins)

  1. Check your analytics from the week
  2. Did you hit your goals?
  3. What went well? What was hard?
  4. Adjust targets for next week based on learnings
  5. Start again with the weekly plan

Week 1 Metrics to Track

  • How many tasks did you complete? (target: 80%+)
  • What was your average completion time?
  • Which time of day were you most productive?
  • What tasks did you avoid or procrastinate?
Remember: Week 1 is about learning your rhythm, not perfection. It's okay if you don't hit 100%!

📈 Advanced Progress Tracking

Level up your productivity with advanced Tenths features.

1. Using Categories & Filters

Organize tasks by area:

  • Academic: All study/learning tasks
  • Professional: Work projects and deadlines
  • Personal: Health, hobbies, self-improvement
  • Financial: Budgeting, spending tracking

2. Custom Metrics

Don't limit yourself to chapters/hours. Try:

  • Miles (running goal)
  • Words (writing project)
  • Dollars (savings goal)
  • Servings (meal prep)
  • Days (streak counter)

3. Parallel Task Tracking

Track multiple projects simultaneously:

  • Create a "master goal" task
  • Create sub-tasks for each component
  • The dashboard shows progress on all of them
  • See which areas need attention

4. Analyzing Your Data

Use analytics to answer:

  • Which category takes up most of my time?
  • What's my realistic pace per day?
  • When do I procrastinate most?
  • Which deadlines do I consistently miss?
  • Am I getting better week over week?

5. Setting Stretch Goals

Once comfortable with basics:

  1. Identify your baseline (what you consistently do)
  2. Set a stretch goal (10-20% higher)
  3. Track if you can sustain it
  4. If yes, make it your new baseline
  5. Set a new stretch goal
Advanced Tip: Export your data monthly and analyze trends. Over time, you'll see how consistent practice builds exponential progress!

🔒 Privacy & Security Settings

Protect your account and understand your privacy options.

1. Password Security

  • Use a strong password (12+ characters, mix of letters/numbers/symbols)
  • Don't share your password with anyone
  • Use a unique password (don't reuse across apps)
  • Change your password every 3 months
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) for extra security

2. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

How to enable:

  1. Go to Settings → Security
  2. Toggle "Two-Factor Authentication"
  3. Follow the on-screen instructions
